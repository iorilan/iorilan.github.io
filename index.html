About .Net coding performance

[GC Basic]

In a managed process there are two types of heaps—native and managed. Native heaps are allocated with the VirtualAlloc Windows API and used by the operating system and CLR for unmanaged memory such as that for the Windows API, OS data structures, and even much of the CLR. The CLR allocates all managed .NET objects on the managed heap, also called the GC heap, because the objects on it are subject to garbage collection.

If it will not fit in an existing spot, then the allocator will expand the current boundaries of gen 0 to accommodate the new object. If this pushes past the end of the segment, it may trigger a garbage collection.

The story about processing as a team

First , lets see where are we

Lets stop arguing what is the best practice and focus on moving forward . if what we are not moving forward everyday , we will be lost in some day.

Second , Where we are going ?

Where is the next stop ? and how does it comes up ?it comes up from a lunch time discussion ? or it is comming after a details planing ? Lets stop for a moment and think about it .
Because what are we going to do is where we are spending our time and money and efforts on .

Everyone have a voice on a solution ? who will be the one make decision.

In a start up , this is happening quite often. again , the important thing is not who is right or wrong , or even best . lets see front and make sure we are agree on going to the same place and then , lets pick a solution and try it again and again and again!

Coding style ? yes , we need it .

This is about every team . everyone may has a different opinion on the codes they are writting . but , what is more important ?
testable ?readability ? maintainence ? performance ? or extensible ?

  • you jumped into a project in the middle , just follow the coding converntion the team is coding . do a interface for anything you dislike , and it’s better to start a new project called “Unit Test” if there is not !
  • you are starting a new project . remember:
    — spending too much time on design but forgot prototype
    — thinking too much on which technology to use to do the same job (in this case , lets start with a interface and do researching on whatever we want to try !)
    — take into everything into account ? forget about it ! now create a prj and put some code make it works then it means we are moving forward.
    — coding is really depends on the team skillset , after everyone agree on the naming converntion, just start. any part you dislike such as moving method into new class , a visitor or command pattern , just change do refactoring job as you like. but dont expect others will do same as you did .
    — but like DI , stairway pattern , or layers , should be clearify in the first time.

We have to rush on a ticket? This is the steps i am following :

-1. finish the feature ,if possible , write unit test after each single step.
-2. go through the logic and add comment where nessacery .
-3. got time to refactor ?
— clean up dependency by DI
— analysis where need to have an interface .
— behaviour and data keep saperate .
— inheritence ? careful . in real world , inheritence is natural .but in programming , inheritence usally done by composition .

About attitude

  • be responsible what we write .no time is not an excuse , keep the refactoring debt in mind is important.
  • watch team steps , what is stopping us from moving forward, point it out!
  • watch yourself , dont be the one draging team behind .

Good Or Bad

About Pattern and Convention

Last week , I was going through one part of codes in project . It consists two hierarchy of classes — bridge pattern . but based on the problem complexity, this bridge pattern brings too much unnecessary complexity. whereas 1 simple class is easier to be changed and much easier for team to read and understand.

Pattern are not wrong . peoples are wrong , put the wrong things on wrong problem domain .patterns is to simplify problem but not to complex problem. If there are rules in software programming, keep it simple should be the first one , I think so.

Good Pattern is always coming from your team, not some other great teams, reason is simple : their team members are not in your team. whichever will speed up your progress and understandable to most of team members ,it is good pattern for now.

Pattern should always change based on your team. seeking for good ways to make team working effective should be something we are always keeping in mind and doing. and same thing apply to convention and technical preference, it should depends on team not individual .

conclusion : Patterns and conventions really depends on the team skill set .There are a couple of ways to make codes easier to be understandable , but the point is , for whom ? for team but not yourself ! different person comes from different background , a person plays well in C make be comfortable with a switch includes 5 cases ,but for some “pure OO programming” guys may prefer to see class hierarchy ( Polymorphism)
